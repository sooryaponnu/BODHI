Supporting countries to advance health equity for persons with disabilities

WHO works closely with ministries of health and relevant stakeholders, including persons with disabilities and their representative organizations, to fully integrate and promote disability inclusion in the health sector and advance health equity for persons with disabilities. We support countries in their work to ensure persons with disabilities:

  • have equitable access to effective health services;
  • are included in health emergencies preparedness and responses
  • can access cross-sectoral public health interventions to achieve the highest attainable standard of health.

WHOâ€™s work focuses on providing technical and practical guidance to countries on how to develop a comprehensive national strategic action plan to advance health equity for persons with disabilities through integration of disability into existing health systems strengthening efforts. The process includes first undertaking a comprehensive situational assessment to identify gaps and opportunities for disability inclusion in the health sector, and then developing an action plan with concrete priority activities and monitoring framework. These activities can be mainstreamed across different areas including governance, financing, physical infrastructure, service delivery, health information systems, etc. WHO supports countries to advance disability inclusion using as a foundation, the primary health care approach, in which countries are already investing and have commited to.

WHO is currently developing a disability guide for action, which provides step-by-step guidance to facilitate advancing disability inclusion in the health sector.
